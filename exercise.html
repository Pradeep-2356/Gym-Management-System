Exercise Guide

1. Push-Up

A classic upper body exercise that targets the chest, shoulders, and triceps.

  • Start in a high plank position, hands placed slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.
  • Lower your body by bending your elbows, keeping them close to your body.
  • Push back up to the starting position, engaging your core throughout.

2. Squat

An effective lower body exercise that strengthens the glutes, quads, and hamstrings.

  • Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, toes slightly turned out.
  • Lower your body by bending your knees and pushing your hips back, keeping your chest up.
  • Return to standing by pressing through your heels.

3. Plank

A core-strengthening exercise that also engages the shoulders, back, and glutes.

  • Start in a forearm plank position, elbows directly under your shoulders.
  • Engage your core and keep your body in a straight line from head to heels.
  • Hold the position for as long as you can without compromising form.

4. Burpees

A full-body exercise that increases your heart rate while building strength and endurance.

  • Start standing, then drop into a squat position with your hands on the ground.
  • Kick your feet back into a plank position.
  • Perform a push-up, then jump your feet back to your hands and jump up.

5. Lunges

A great exercise for strengthening the legs and improving balance.

  • Stand tall and take a step forward with one leg.
  • Lower your hips until both knees are bent at about a 90-degree angle.
  • Return to standing and repeat on the other side.

6. Mountain Climbers

A cardio exercise that engages multiple muscle groups, improving strength and endurance.

  • Start in a high plank position.
  • Drive one knee towards your chest, then switch legs quickly.
  • Continue alternating legs at a fast pace.

7. Glute Bridge

A great exercise to strengthen the glutes and lower back.

  • Lie on your back with knees bent and feet flat on the floor, hip-width apart.
  • Press through your heels to lift your hips towards the ceiling, squeezing your glutes.
  • Lower back down and repeat.

8. Dead Bug

An effective core exercise that also improves coordination.

  • Lie on your back with arms extended toward the ceiling and knees bent at 90 degrees.
  • Lower one arm and the opposite leg towards the floor, keeping your back flat.
  • Return to the starting position and repeat on the other side.

9. Tricep Dips

A targeted exercise for the triceps, also engaging the shoulders and chest.

  • Find a sturdy bench or chair and sit on the edge, hands next to your hips.
  • Slide off the edge and lower your body by bending your elbows to about 90 degrees.
  • Push back up to the starting position.

10. Bicycle Crunches

A powerful core exercise that targets the abs and obliques.

  • Lie on your back with your hands behind your head and knees bent.
  • Lift your shoulder blades off the ground and bring one elbow towards the opposite knee while straightening the other leg.
  • Switch sides in a pedaling motion, engaging your core throughout.

11. Side Plank

A core and oblique strengthening exercise.

  • Lie on your side with your legs straight and feet stacked on top of each other.
  • Prop yourself up on your elbow, lifting your hips off the ground.
  • Hold the position, keeping your body in a straight line.

12. Russian Twists

An effective exercise for strengthening the core and improving rotational movement.

  • Sit on the floor with your knees bent and lean back slightly.
  • Hold your hands together and twist your torso to the right, then to the left.
  • For added intensity, hold a weight or medicine ball.

13. High Knees

A cardio exercise that also engages the core and legs.

  • Stand tall with feet hip-width apart.
  • Run in place, driving your knees up towards your chest.
  • Keep your arms bent at 90 degrees and pump them as you run.

14. Wall Sit

A static exercise that builds endurance in the legs.

  • Stand with your back against a wall, feet shoulder-width apart.
  • Slide down the wall until your thighs are parallel to the ground.
  • Hold the position for as long as you can.

15. Jumping Jacks

A simple yet effective cardio exercise that works the whole body.

  • Start standing tall with feet together and arms at your sides.
  • Jump up, spreading your legs shoulder-width apart while raising your arms overhead.
  • Jump back to the starting position and repeat.
